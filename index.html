Cities I have been to.
The ones worth knowing.

I travel a lot and people ask me where to go and what to do. This is my running list. No fluff, just what I have found works.

🇬🇧   Where I live London

I live here so take this with that in mind. London is enormous and people often see very little of it. The tourist centre is real but it is a small fraction of what the city actually is.

Getting around

Get an Oyster card or tap your contactless bank card on the yellow readers. Do not buy paper single tickets, they cost almost double. Daily and weekly caps mean you never pay more than a set amount however many times you travel. Zones matter: most places visitors want to go are in zones 1 and 2.

01

Columbia Road Flower Market

Sunday mornings only, done by 2pm. East London at its best. Walk up to Broadway Market after for coffee and food stalls.

02

Maltby Street Market

Saturday and Sunday mornings under the railway arches in Bermondsey. Better food than Borough Market and far fewer people.

03

The Barbican

Brutalist architecture with a fantastic arts centre, a conservatory full of tropical plants, and a lake. Very few tourists find it. Worth going in even if you have no interest in concrete.

04

Hampstead Heath

360 hectares of open land with ponds you can swim in. Go up to Parliament Hill for views over the whole city.

05

Peckham Rye

South-east London neighbourhood that has changed a lot in ten years. Good independent restaurants, Frank's rooftop bar in summer, and views of the city from a multi-storey car park.

06

V&A Museum

Free entry. The building itself is worth a visit. Spend half a day in the ceramics and fashion collections or go straight to the Islamic art rooms which most people walk straight past.

More to add including areas to stay, good restaurants by neighbourhood, and day trips.

🇪🇸   Lived here Barcelona

I lived here for a while and still go back. It is the kind of city where you stop noticing the architecture after a week because it is everywhere. The thing most visitors miss is how different each neighbourhood feels from the next.

The travel card (do this first)

Get the Hola BCN! card (available in 2, 3, 4 or 5 consecutive days). It covers metro, bus, tram, FGC suburban trains, and Rodalies including the airport connection on Metro L9 Sud from T1 and T2. It is very cheap compared to single tickets and the airport trip alone nearly pays for a day card. Buy it at any metro station or at the airport on arrival. For a short visit with a group, the T-Casual (10 trips, shareable between people travelling together) is another good option.

01

Gracia neighbourhood

Go here to see what Barcelona is actually like. Small squares, local shops, tables outside in the evening. Walk up from Passeig de Gracia and you leave the tourists behind quickly.

02

Mercat de Santa Caterina

Go here instead of La Boqueria. Locals still use it, the food is just as good, and you can actually move. The tiled roof by Enric Miralles is one of the best things in the city.

03

El Born

Medieval streets, good bars, the Picasso Museum, and the Basilica de Santa Maria del Mar. Walk around in the morning before it fills up.

04

Montjuic

Take the cable car up. The Fundacio Joan Miro is there and is very good. Good views over the port. The Olympic stadium is easy to walk to and free to enter.

05

Barceloneta to Poblenou walk

Walk north along the beach from Barceloneta. About 40 minutes. The Rambla del Poblenou at the end is where people actually go in the evenings, not La Rambla in the centre.

🇬🇷   Lived here Athens

Athens is not just the Acropolis. That is obvious but still true. The city has neighbourhoods that feel completely different from each other and most visitors never get past the main sites. Give it at least four days.

01

Anafiotika

A cluster of white houses wedged into the north slope of the Acropolis rock. Looks like a Cycladic village dropped into the city. Quiet, cats everywhere. Most visitors walk straight past the entrance.

02

Monastiraki flea market

Sunday mornings. Vinyl, old maps, vintage cameras, metalwork, junk, the occasional good find. Get there before noon. The souvlaki stalls around the square open early.

03

Exarchia

It has a reputation but it is fine during the day and has some of the best cheap food in the city. Independent bookshops, political murals, very different atmosphere from the tourist areas.

04

Cape Sounion

Temple of Poseidon on a cliff 70km from Athens. Take the KTEL coastal bus, the road hugs the cliffs the whole way. Go for sunset. Byron carved his name into one of the columns.

05

National Archaeological Museum

One of the best museums in the world and often not crowded. The Antikythera Mechanism is there, a bronze computing device from 70BC that should not exist. Go and see it.

🇦🇱   Multiple visits Albania

Almost nobody goes and that is starting to change. Ottoman old towns, mountains that rival anywhere in Europe, an Ionian coast that is better than the Greek islands in July and August because it has fewer people, and prices that feel like a different era. Go soon.

Practical notes

Currency is the Lek (ALL). Cards are increasingly accepted in cities but keep cash for rural areas and smaller guesthouses. Roads in the south have improved a lot but mountain passes take longer than maps suggest. Build in extra time.

01

Berat

UNESCO town called the city of a thousand windows. The Ottoman quarter climbs the hillside above a river. Stay overnight, the light in the evening is different.

02

Gjirokaster

Another UNESCO old town with a fortress above the valley. Birthplace of both Enver Hoxha and Ismail Kadare. The bazaar quarter is well preserved.

03

Valbona to Theth hike

The Albanian Alps. A full day hike between these two villages, one of the best mountain walks in Europe. Book guesthouses ahead in summer.

04

Ksamil and the Riviera

Ionian coast from Sarande to Himara. Turquoise water, small beaches, a fraction of the crowds you get across the border in Greece.

05

Butrint

Greek, Roman, Byzantine and Venetian ruins in a lagoon setting near Sarande. One of the most atmospheric archaeological sites in the Balkans.

06

Shkoder

Northern city near Lake Shkodra and Montenegro. Rozafa Castle is worth the climb. Good fish restaurants on the water.

07

Tirana

Worth two days. Blloku neighbourhood for cafes and nightlife. The painted building facades were ordered by the mayor in 2000 to cheer the city up.

08

Apollonia

Greek colony ruins on a hill above olive groves near Fier. Very quiet. A good stop on the way between Tirana and the south.

09

Permet and the Vjosa

A town in a river valley surrounded by mountains. The Vjosa is one of the last undammed wild rivers in Europe. Best in spring and autumn.

10

Durres

Main port and nearest beach city to Tirana. A Roman amphitheatre in the middle of the city that was only rediscovered in the 1960s. Enormous and largely unexcavated.
