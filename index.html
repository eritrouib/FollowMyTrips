Cities I have been to.
The ones worth knowing.

I travel a lot and people ask me where to go and what to do. This is my running list. No fluff, just what I have found works.

🇬🇧   Where I live London

I live here so take this with that in mind. London is enormous and people often see very little of it. The tourist centre is real but it is a small fraction of what the city actually is.

Getting around

Get an Oyster card or tap your contactless bank card on the yellow readers. Do not buy paper single tickets, they cost almost double. Daily and weekly caps mean you never pay more than a set amount however many times you travel. Zones matter: most places visitors want to go are in zones 1 and 2.

01

Columbia Road Flower Market

Sunday mornings only, done by 2pm. East London at its best. Walk up to Broadway Market after for coffee and food stalls.

02

Maltby Street Market

Saturday and Sunday mornings under the railway arches in Bermondsey. Better food than Borough Market and far fewer people.

03

The Barbican

Brutalist architecture with a fantastic arts centre, a conservatory full of tropical plants, and a lake. Very few tourists find it. Worth going in even if you have no interest in concrete.

04

Hampstead Heath

360 hectares of open land with ponds you can swim in. Go up to Parliament Hill for views over the whole city.

05

Peckham Rye

South-east London neighbourhood that has changed a lot in ten years. Good independent restaurants, Frank's rooftop bar in summer, and views of the city from a multi-storey car park.

06

V&A Museum

Free entry. The building itself is worth a visit. Spend half a day in the ceramics and fashion collections or go straight to the Islamic art rooms which most people walk straight past.

🇪🇸   3 visits Barcelona

I have been three times and would go back again. The Modernisme architecture is everywhere so you stop being surprised by it after a day, which is a good thing. Each neighbourhood has a different feel and it is worth getting out of the centre.

Get the Hola Barcelona Travel Card first

The Hola Barcelona Travel Card covers metro, bus, tram, FGC and Rodalies trains, and the airport connection on Metro L9 Sud from T1 and T2. It does not cover the private Aerobus but you do not need it. Available for 2, 3, 4 or 5 consecutive days. Buy it at any metro station or at the airport on arrival. On public buses you show the card rather than tap it on the machine. If you go to Tibidabo it covers the lower funicular only.

On food

Food anywhere away from the very touristy areas is noticeably better and cheaper. Do not sit at the restaurants on Las Ramblas. More expensive, smaller portions, and not great food. Walk one street back and things improve immediately.

01

Sagrada Familia

I have not been inside but many people say it is worth it. Book tickets well ahead online, the queues without a ticket are very long.

02

Casa Mila and Casa Batllo

Both are on opposite sides of Passeig de Gracia. Walk between them, keep going towards Placa de Catalunya and you reach La Rambla and the Mercat de la Boqueria from there.

03

Gothic Quarter and Cathedral

Worth a proper walk around. The Barcelona Cathedral is at the heart of it. Easy to spend a couple of hours just wandering the streets.

04

Parc Guell

Beautiful park with good views over the city. The main Monumental Zone requires a ticket, the rest of the park is free. Go in the morning before it fills up.

05

Montjuic Castle

More worth it if the Font Magica fountain is running and you combine it with a tour. There is a cable car up and it may be included in the Barcelona card but only at specific times, so check before you go.

06

Arc de Triomf to Ciutadella

Walk from Arc de Triomf down the tree-lined boulevard and into Parc de la Ciutadella. A good way to spend a morning.

07

Barceloneta

Walk along the beach. Plenty of restaurants along the seafront with decent food. A much better option than eating on Las Ramblas.

08

Tibidabo

Go for the view. The amusement park is very small and not really the reason to make the trip up. The lower funicular is covered by the Hola BCN card.

Day trips (Sitges, Montserrat), more on neighbourhoods and where to eat to come.

🇬🇷   Lived here Athens

Athens is not just the Acropolis. That is obvious but still true. The city has neighbourhoods that feel completely different from each other and most visitors never get past the main sites. Give it at least four days.

01

Anafiotika

A cluster of white houses wedged into the north slope of the Acropolis rock. Looks like a Cycladic village dropped into the city. Quiet, cats everywhere. Most visitors walk straight past the entrance.

02

Monastiraki flea market

Sunday mornings. Vinyl, old maps, vintage cameras, metalwork, junk, the occasional good find. Get there before noon. The souvlaki stalls around the square open early.

03

Exarchia

It has a reputation but it is fine during the day and has some of the best cheap food in the city. Independent bookshops, political murals, very different atmosphere from the tourist areas.

04

Cape Sounion

Temple of Poseidon on a cliff 70km from Athens. Take the KTEL coastal bus, the road hugs the cliffs the whole way. Go for sunset. Byron carved his name into one of the columns.

05

National Archaeological Museum

One of the best museums in the world and often not crowded. The Antikythera Mechanism is there, a bronze computing device from 70BC that should not exist. Go and see it.

Day trips, island ferries, where to eat by neighbourhood, and getting around Athens to come.

🇦🇱   Lived in Elbasan + multiple visits Albania

I grew up in Elbasan and have been back many times. Almost nobody from outside visits and that is starting to change. Ottoman old towns, mountains that rival anywhere in Europe, an Ionian coast with fewer people than Greece in summer, and prices that feel like a different era.

Practical notes

Currency is the Lek (ALL). Cards are increasingly accepted in cities but keep cash for rural areas and smaller guesthouses. Roads in the south have improved a lot but mountain passes take longer than maps suggest. Build in extra time. If driving, be extra cautious even on highways as there can be animals or people crossing out of nowhere.

Coastal restaurants

In Himara and coastal towns generally, restaurants start closing from early October after the long summer season. If you are planning to visit in autumn, check ahead before making a trip to a specific place.

01

Tirana

Worth two days minimum. Walk from the Skenderbeu monument towards the University to see the ministries. Visit Pazari i Ri (the New Bazar) for lunch with traditional food. The Pyramid is a trendy place to climb on top of. For the evening, Blloku is the neighbourhood to go to. If you have time, there is a cable car to Mount Dajt. For a restaurant, Mrizi i Zanave is well known and has everything bio with a village atmosphere, though it is on the expensive side. mriziizanave.al

02

BunkArt 1 and BunkArt 2

Two museums inside actual Cold War bunkers, both in Tirana. They show what life was like during communism in Albania. BunkArt 1 is the larger one, built into a mountain. BunkArt 2 is in the centre. Both are worth visiting.

03

Berat

UNESCO town called the city of a thousand windows. The Ottoman quarter climbs the hillside above a river. Stay overnight, the light in the evening is different.

04

Gjirokaster

Another UNESCO old town with a fortress above the valley. Birthplace of both Enver Hoxha and Ismail Kadare. The bazaar quarter is well preserved. Ask locally for good restaurants.

05

Valbona to Theth hike

The Albanian Alps. A full day hike between these two villages, one of the best mountain walks in Europe. Book guesthouses well ahead in summer.

06

Ksamil and the Riviera

Ionian coast from Sarande to Himara. Turquoise water, small beaches, a fraction of the crowds you get across the border in Greece.

07

Butrint

Greek, Roman, Byzantine and Venetian ruins in a lagoon setting near Sarande. One of the most atmospheric archaeological sites in the Balkans and usually quiet.

08

Shkoder

Northern city near Lake Shkodra and Montenegro. Rozafa Castle is worth the climb. Good fish restaurants on the water.

09

Elbasan

One of the oldest towns in Albania. The Elbasan Castle has a nice history and is worth visiting. Do not eat at the restaurant inside the castle though, it is overpriced and not good. In town, Taverna Attika is well known for Greek food and has great quality, though prices reflect that.

10

Apollonia

Greek colony ruins on a hill above olive groves near Fier. Very quiet. A good stop on the way between Tirana and the south.

11

Permet and the Vjosa

A town in a river valley surrounded by mountains. The Vjosa is one of the last undammed wild rivers in Europe. Best in spring and autumn.

12

Durres

Main port and nearest beach city to Tirana. A Roman amphitheatre in the middle of the city, only rediscovered in the 1960s. Enormous and largely unexcavated. On the way back to Tirana from the south, take the route through Durres rather than the direct road.

13

Lake Komani

In north Albania. There is a boat cruise on the lake which many people say is worth it. I have not been myself as I have seen very little of northern Albania, but it comes up constantly as a recommendation.
