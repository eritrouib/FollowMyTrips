🇦🇱   Grew up in Elbasan
Albania
← All cities

I grew up in Elbasan and have been back many times. Albania has an ancient history that goes back well before any empire passed through it, and most visitors only scratch the surface. Fortified hilltop towns, Greek and Roman ruins, mountains that rival anywhere in Europe, an Ionian coast with fewer people than Greece in summer, and prices that are still very reasonable.

Before you go

Practical notes

Currency is the Lek (ALL). Cards are increasingly accepted in cities but keep cash for rural areas and smaller guesthouses. Roads in the south have improved a lot but mountain passes take longer than maps suggest. Build in extra time. If driving, be extra cautious even on highways as there can be animals or people crossing out of nowhere.

Coastal restaurants in autumn

In Himara and coastal towns generally, restaurants start closing from early October after the long summer season. If you are planning to visit in autumn, check ahead before making a trip to a specific place.

Places worth going

01

Tirana

Worth two days minimum. Walk from the Skenderbeu monument towards the University to see the ministries. Visit Pazari i Ri (the New Bazar) for lunch. The Pyramid is a trendy place to climb. For the evening, Blloku is the neighbourhood to go to. If you have time, there is a cable car to Mount Dajt. For a restaurant, Mrizi i Zanave has everything bio with a village atmosphere, though it is on the expensive side. mriziizanave.al

02

BunkArt 1 and 2

Two museums inside actual Cold War bunkers in Tirana. They show what life was like during communism in Albania. BunkArt 1 is larger, built into a mountain. BunkArt 2 is in the centre. Both worth visiting.

03

Berat

UNESCO town called the city of a thousand windows. The old quarter climbs the hillside above the Osum river. Stay overnight, the light in the evening is different.

04

Gjirokaster

UNESCO listed old town with a fortress above the valley. Birthplace of both Enver Hoxha and Ismail Kadare. The stone-paved bazaar quarter is well preserved. Ask locally for good restaurants.

05

Valbona to Theth hike

The Albanian Alps. A full day hike between these two villages, one of the best mountain walks in Europe. Book guesthouses well ahead in summer.

06

Ksamil and the Riviera

Ionian coast from Sarande to Himara. Turquoise water, small beaches, a fraction of the crowds you get across the border in Greece.

07

Butrint

Greek, Roman, Byzantine and Venetian ruins in a lagoon setting near Sarande. One of the most atmospheric archaeological sites in the Balkans and usually quiet.

08

Shkoder

Northern city near Lake Shkodra and Montenegro. Rozafa Castle is worth the climb. Good fish restaurants on the water.

09

Elbasan

One of the oldest towns in Albania. The Elbasan Castle has a rich history. Do not eat at the restaurant inside the castle though, it is overpriced and not good. In town, Taverna Attika is well known for Greek food and has great quality, though prices reflect that.

10

Apollonia

Greek colony ruins on a hill above olive groves near Fier. Very quiet. A good stop on the way between Tirana and the south.

11

Permet and the Vjosa

A town in a river valley surrounded by mountains. The Vjosa is one of the last undammed wild rivers in Europe. Best in spring and autumn.

12

Durres

Main port and nearest beach city to Tirana. A Roman amphitheatre in the middle of the city, only rediscovered in the 1960s. On the way back to Tirana from the south, take the route through Durres rather than the direct road.

13

Lake Komani

In north Albania. A boat cruise on the lake which many people say is worth it. I have not been myself as I have seen very little of northern Albania, but it comes up constantly as a recommendation.
