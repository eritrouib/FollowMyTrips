I live in London and have travelled around the UK quite a bit. It is a much more varied country than people expect.

🇬🇧 Where I live

London, United Kingdom

London is enormous and people often see very little of it. Before I moved here I visited several times, and my European friends have all said the same thing: they expected one city and found something much bigger.

Where to stay

Stay as close to the centre as your budget allows. The money you think you save on a cheaper hotel further out you will spend on TfL. Zones 1 and 2 is the sweet spot.

Getting around

Tap your contactless bank card on the yellow readers. Do not buy paper single tickets, they cost almost double. If you have Revolut, use it. Most European banks charge transaction fees. Revolut does not.

Museums are free

Almost all major museums have free entry. The Natural History Museum, V&A, British Museum, Tate Modern, National Gallery, Science Museum. All free. My favourites are the Natural History Museum and the Tate.

Places worth going

01

Soho

Where theatres, restaurants, bars and independent shops all run into each other. Best in the evening. visitlondon.com guide

02

West End theatres

One of the best concentrations of theatre in the world. Book ahead for the big shows, or get same-day discounted tickets at the TKTS booth in Leicester Square.

03

Natural History Museum

Free entry. The building alone is worth the visit. Give it half a day minimum.

04

Tate Modern

Free entry. On the South Bank with good views across the Thames to St Paul's.

05

Richmond Park

2,500 acres of ancient parkland with wild deer that walk past you at close range. Take the District line to Richmond.

06

Columbia Road Flower Market

Sunday mornings only, done by 2pm. Walk to Broadway Market after for coffee and food stalls.

07

The Barbican

1960s brutalist architecture with a fantastic arts centre, a conservatory full of tropical plants, and a lake. Very few tourists find it.

08

Hampstead Heath

360 hectares of open land with ponds you can swim in. Parliament Hill has views across the whole city.
