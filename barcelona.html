Barcelona
← All cities

I have been three times and would go back again. The Modernisme architecture is everywhere so you stop being surprised by it after a day, which is a good thing. Each neighbourhood has a different feel and it is worth getting out of the centre.

Getting around

Get the Hola Barcelona Travel Card first

The Hola Barcelona Travel Card covers metro, bus, tram, FGC and Rodalies trains, and the airport connection on Metro L9 Sud from T1 and T2. It does not cover the private Aerobus but you do not need it. Available for 2, 3, 4 or 5 consecutive days. Buy it at any metro station or at the airport on arrival. On public buses you show the card rather than tap it. If you go to Tibidabo it covers the lower funicular only.

On food

Food anywhere away from the very touristy areas is noticeably better and cheaper. Do not sit at the restaurants on Las Ramblas. More expensive, smaller portions, and not great food. Walk one street back and things improve immediately.

Places worth going

01

Sagrada Familia

I have not been inside but many people say it is worth it. Book tickets well ahead online, the queues without a ticket are very long.

02

Casa Mila and Casa Batllo

Both are on opposite sides of Passeig de Gracia. Walk between them, keep going towards Placa de Catalunya and you reach La Rambla and the Mercat de la Boqueria from there.

03

Gothic Quarter and Cathedral

Worth a proper walk around. The Barcelona Cathedral is at the heart of it. Easy to spend a couple of hours just wandering the streets.

04

Parc Guell

Beautiful park with good views over the city. The main Monumental Zone requires a ticket, the rest of the park is free. Go in the morning before it fills up.

05

Montjuic Castle

More worth it if the Font Magica fountain is running and you combine it with a tour. There is a cable car up and it may be included in the Barcelona card but only at specific times, so check before you go.

06

Arc de Triomf to Ciutadella

Walk from Arc de Triomf down the tree-lined boulevard and into Parc de la Ciutadella. A good way to spend a morning.

07

Barceloneta

Walk along the beach. Plenty of restaurants along the seafront with decent food. A much better option than eating on Las Ramblas.

08

Tibidabo

Go for the view. The amusement park is very small and not really the reason to make the trip up. The lower funicular is covered by the Hola BCN card.
