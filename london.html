🇬🇧   Where I live
London
← All cities

I live here so take this with that in mind. London is enormous and people often see very little of it. Before I moved here I visited several times, and my European friends who have come to visit have all said the same thing: they expected one city and found something much bigger and harder to categorise.

Before you arrive

Where to stay

Stay as close to the centre as your budget allows. The money you think you save on a cheaper hotel further out you will spend on TfL getting in and out. Zones 1 and 2 is the sweet spot. Anything in zone 3 or beyond starts adding up fast if you are visiting for only a few days.

Getting around

You do not need an Oyster card. Just tap your contactless bank card directly on the yellow readers at the gate or on the bus. Daily and weekly caps apply, so you never pay more than a set amount however many trips you make. If you have a Revolut card, use it. Most European banks charge transaction fees in a foreign currency. Revolut does not, which makes a real difference over a few days. Do not buy paper single tickets under any circumstances, they cost almost double.

Museums are free

Almost all the major museums in London have free entry. The Natural History Museum, the V&A, the British Museum, the Tate Modern, the National Gallery, the Science Museum. All free. This is unusual for a capital city and worth planning your time around. My favourites are the Natural History Museum and the Tate.

Places worth going

01

Soho

Where theatres, restaurants, bars, and independent shops all run into each other. Good at any time of day but best in the evening. visitlondon.com has a good area guide.

02

West End theatres

London has one of the best concentrations of theatre in the world. Book ahead for the big shows, but same-day discounted tickets are at the TKTS booth in Leicester Square if you are flexible.

03

Natural History Museum

Free entry. The building alone is worth the visit. The dinosaur skeleton in the main hall, the blue whale, the Darwin Centre. Give it half a day minimum.

04

Tate Modern

Free entry. On the South Bank with good views across the Thames to St Paul's. The permanent collection is excellent and the temporary exhibitions usually worth seeing too.

05

Richmond Park

2,500 acres of ancient parkland with wild red and fallow deer that walk past you at close range. It does not feel like London at all. Take the District line to Richmond and walk in from there.

06

Columbia Road Flower Market

Sunday mornings only, done by 2pm. East London at its best. Walk up to Broadway Market after for coffee and food stalls.

07

The Barbican

1960s brutalist architecture with a fantastic arts centre, a conservatory full of tropical plants, and a lake. Very few tourists find it.

08

Hampstead Heath

360 hectares of open land with ponds you can swim in. Go up to Parliament Hill for views across the whole city.
