Athens
← All cities

Athens is not just the Acropolis. That is obvious but still true. The city has neighbourhoods that feel completely different from each other and most visitors never get past the main sites. Give it at least four days.

Where to eat

O Thanasis

One of the most well known souvlaki restaurants in Athens, in Monastiraki. It has been there for decades and the food is consistently good. Go for the kebab and the grilled meat. Read more about it or find it on maps.

Places worth going

01

Anafiotika

A cluster of white houses wedged into the north slope of the Acropolis rock. Looks like a Cycladic village dropped into the city. Quiet, cats everywhere. Most visitors walk straight past the entrance.

02

Monastiraki flea market

Sunday mornings. Vinyl, old maps, vintage cameras, metalwork, junk, the occasional good find. Get there before noon. The souvlaki stalls around the square open early.

03

Exarchia

It has a reputation but it is fine during the day and has some of the best cheap food in the city. Independent bookshops, political murals, very different atmosphere from the tourist areas.

04

Cape Sounion

Temple of Poseidon on a cliff 70km from Athens. Take the KTEL coastal bus, the road hugs the cliffs the whole way. Go for sunset. Byron carved his name into one of the columns.

05

National Archaeological Museum

One of the best museums in the world and often not crowded. The Antikythera Mechanism is there, a bronze computing device from 70BC that should not exist. Go and see it.
